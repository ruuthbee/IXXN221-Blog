Week Two: Understanding users

Persona Workshop

Personas are great tools to use when designing as it allows you to refer back to the intended user and make sure your design has them considered in the center. It is definitely a struggle to create a persona that actually feels like a real person and not make it too stereotypical while still believable.

I personally struggled to come up with a persona before creating a design. This is something I want to be aware of because the design should be molded around the person and not the other way around. People shouldn’t be accommodating the design, the design should be accommodating them. I think that not considering the different pain points and motivations of people could lead to groups of people who have more unique struggles being left out pf consideration when designs are being created. It would be easy to say that they are just not the intended recipient of the design, but if there is not alternative that caters to their needs, what are they left with? Considering how the internet has almost always had goals of developing communities and is ever becoming more and more essential to becoming involved in community and creating a relationships (virtual and physical), people who are unable to participate due to accessibility issues not being considered in the design, missing out on the same level of connection.

Goal Setting

  • SMART goals, qualitative and quantitative goals

  • “what do you want to ensure about the user experience of that feature or functionality?”

  • purpose = determine user behaviors that act as a signal for that goal.

Venn Diagrams

  • Venn diagrams are great! but can also be complicated.

  • Venn diagrams are used to map out the users’ goals and the design goals in relation to each other. It highlights what goals are specific to the individual and what are the shared goals between the two

  • One key take away from class is that it’s important to note the key players of your Venn diagrams, because sometimes we have the goals of the developer/designer, the user, AND the company or institution.

Week Four: Journey Map & User Flows

Web History 2008 - 2022

Over this period wed design trends changed to flat designs and corporate Memphis illstrations

We see a great example of memphis illustrations used by Hinge to the right.

Trends are constantly changing and as the scene gets more and more saturated by a certain trend, like memphis illustrations, we'll see a shift in what's considered popular, more exciting, and innovative.

As a designer I think it is important to be aware of trends and how to use them but to not stop yourself from going beyond them. As trends only last so long and fresh ideas will replace them.

Customer Journey maps and User Flows:

  • Customer Journey maps specifically outline the actions, thoughts, and emotions of the user throughout the different stages of the interaction with product/brand.

  • User Flows show the flow of the website and points of active interaction the user must go through.

  • These are important tool for designers to use to gain insight into their users perspectives!

Week Six: Assignment 2.1 intro, colour & typography

Colour

  • RGB
    - mainly used for digital formats.
    - Additive colour model.

  • CMYK
    - mainly used for print formats.
    - Subtractive colour model.

  • Pantone
    Standardised system for identifying and matching colours.

I've used all three of these colour systems. I find it easiest to use hex codes which is great for digital formats like a website.

I'm not sure what type of colour scheme my website design will have but most likely analogous or complementary. I like the idea of haveing one or two accent colours with a high contrast. This also should make sure that my colour scheme has good accessibility for people with vision impairments.

My colour tones should be friendly, relate to nature, and inspirational.

Assignment Reflection

  • Making things responsive is hard. There are a lot of different moving parts to think about. I think I have a new found appreciation for websites that are fully responsive in ways that work well!

  • I feel that I understand how grids and flex boxes work much better. My website design required quite a few problem solving sessions but in the end I was getting much more profficient at creating grid layouts that were responsive. The most challenged part to make responsive would be the about section and the map with the region buttons over top. This is because the background images are organically shaped and would move around underneath the text/buttons when the browser size was changed. This was particularly problematic for the about section because it meant that the text was not always reable. I managed to find a solution by changing the measurement for the text to a responsive one, rather than using px, and creating a margin and padding to make sure the text stayed inside the shape.
